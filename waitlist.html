Pilot focus

Who

Wrestling, BJJ, judo, MMA, and serious grappling athletes in the Bay Area.

What

Early matching logic, open-mat discovery, clinic surfacing, organizer intake, and trust-layer demand capture.

Where

Hayward, Oakland, Berkeley, San Francisco, San Jose, Palo Alto, Walnut Creek, and nearby hubs.

Stage

Concierge alpha first. Tight region, narrow scope, then software only if the signal is strong.

Future product layers

The alpha starts narrow, but the product can grow into a full local combat-sports operating surface.

These are roadmap layers, not promises. The goal is to validate the local network first and only then decide what deserves software.

Core

Partner fit engine

Weight, age band, size, skill, partner preference, style, and travel radius as first-class matching constraints.

Layer

Open mats and trial passes

A cleaner way for gyms to surface good sessions and for athletes to find the right room without noise.

Layer

Clinics and organizers

Hosts can list clinics, specialty sessions, camps, and low-cost events only after local demand is real.

Premium

Trust and verification

Higher-trust intros, optional verification, and safer coordination as a premium layer once the base network exists.

Support

Sponsor and athlete support

Sponsor interest, donation tiers, and athlete support are possible later once there is enough real signal and trust.

Boundary

Separate from TMA

This stays on the commercial/product side, not the nonprofit side, especially for anything experimental or monetized.
