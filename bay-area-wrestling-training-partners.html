Bay Area guide

Bay Area wrestling training partners should not be this hard to find.

Most Bay Area athletes still find training partners through random posts, gym friendships, or direct messages. Training Partner is Milo's attempt to build a better local matching surface around the variables that actually matter: weight, size, skill, age band, travel radius, room feel, and intensity.

Problem

Partner matching breaks when location is the only filter.

01

Wrong size

If the size gap is too wide, the round is often useless for one athlete and unsafe for the other.

02

Wrong level

Beginners need teaching signal, advanced athletes need real pace, and competitive athletes need hard rounds on purpose.

03

Wrong room

The Bay Area has good rooms, but discovery is fragmented. Open mats, clinics, and trial sessions still depend too much on chance.

How Milo is framing it

Training Partner treats local combat-sports discovery like a matching problem, not just a directory.

This is still alpha. The live surface today is intake and signal capture, not a finished marketplace.

Match

Weight and size

Approximate class, body size, and whether the athlete wants equal rounds, bigger rounds, or smaller technical rounds.

Match

Skill and intent

Beginner, intermediate, advanced, or competition-focused plus what the athlete actually wants from the session.

Match

Travel radius

Some athletes want a five-mile room. Others will drive across the Bay for the right practice.

Layer

Room discovery

Good open mats, drop-ins, and clinics should be discoverable in the same place as partner matching.
